Resource Allocation Games

Equations

Equations is the game of creative mathematics. Players use various mathematics to come up with equivalent expressions. A player sets a goal consisting of numbers and/or operations and all players create an equation that equals the goal using numbers and operations.

Required Game Equipment

  • Game Board
  • 1 minute timer
  • Challenge block
  • 6 red cubes
  • 6 blue cubes
  • 6 green cubes
  • 6 black cubes

On-Sets

On-Sets is a game that involves Set Theory. Players utilize set operations to identify groups of color coded cards that form sets. A player sets a numerical goal and all players come up with a set, using colors and set operations, that has a cardinality equal to the goal.

Required Game Equipment

  • Game Board
  • 1 minute timer
  • Challenge block
  • 16 color cards
  • 3 digit cubes
  • 8 color cubes
  • 4 operation cubes
  • 3 restriction cubes

WFF 'N PROOF

Wff N' Proof is a game of symbolic logic where students must construct proofs to prove logical arguments that they create. A player sets a logic statement on the goal and all players create premise and prove that the premise implies the goal.

Required Game Equipment

  • Game Board
  • 1 minute timer
  • Challenge block
  • 14 Upper Case Cubes (C, A, K, E, N)
  • 14 Lower Case Cubes (i, o, p, q, r ,s)

LinguiSHTIK

LinguiSHTIK is an English Language Arts game, where students must construct a particular type of sentence and locate a type of word within the sentence. Players write sentences with demands placed on the grammar and vocabulary by each player.

Required Game Equipment

  • Playing Mat
  • 1 minute timer
  • Challenge block
  • 4 black cubes
  • 4 red cubes
  • 4 green cubes
  • 4 yellow cubes
  • 4 pink cubes
  • 3 orange cubes

On-Words

On-Words is a word game involving spelling, counting, grammar, phonetics, word roots, inflectional endings, prefixes and suffixes. A player sets a numerical goal and players try to come up with a word or network that has the same number of letters as the goal.

Required Game Equipment

  • Game Board
  • 1 minute timer
  • Challenge block
  • 6 black cubes (E, I, N, O, R, T)
  • 4 blue cubes (A, C, D, E, L, S)
  • 4 red cubes (A, F, H, M, P, U)
  • 3 green cubes (B, E, G, T, V, W)
  • 2 pink cubes (I, N, O, R, S, Y)
  • 2 yellow cubes (J, K, Q, X, Y, Z)
  • 3 number cubes
  • 7 orange phonetics cubes (including h, ô, t, s, ou, n, d)

Reading Games

Propaganda

Propaganda is a game that assesses student's knowledge of recognizing and understanding different techniques of persuasion. Players are asked to identify techniques of propaganda that are being used in politics, advertisements, and everyday life.

Required Game Equipment

  • Reader
  • Pen
  • Paper
  • Technique List

Presidents

Presidents is an American History game that assesses students' knowledge of the American Presidents, and the events that occurred during their presidencies. Students identify U.S Presidents based on clues that contain information about a specific president.

Required Game Equipment

  • Reader
  • Pen
  • Paper
  • Presidents Chart
  • 6-4-2 Chart

Theme

Theme is a game where players learn to research a particular historical theme that varies from year to year and to prepare notes and study materials about that theme. Examples of past themes include “Women in World History,” “The 1960’s,” “The American West,” and “Space Exploration.” Players learn how the events in the theme affected people at the time and how those issues still affect them today. The knowledge gained from playing Theme leads to more informed and responsible future citizens.

Required Game Equipment

  • Reader
  • Pen
  • Paper

Current Events

Current Events is a game where players learn about the major political and cultural events of the most recent calendar year. They learn to be aware of what is happening to them, their country, and their world.

Required Game Equipment

  • Reader
  • Pen
  • Paper
